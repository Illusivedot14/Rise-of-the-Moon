Sindral, on the island of Dral along the western shores of Ere'shin, is one of the most magical cities on the continent. Before the discovery of the great anchor of Conjuration, Dral was a patchwork of tribal territories. However, the crystal had shown them how to create, and more importantly, share amongst each other. The city grew into a single vast circle, the whole island its tower, and its exports, crystal-charged items and the services of its mages, made Sindral one of the strongest nations in the world.

The Kraken Sin guarded the waters around Dral. Any ship carrying raw crystal was intercepted, by storm or by tentacle; nothing of the anchor left the island. Sindral found a way to move the crystal off the island with the help of King Caelin of Armestria. In response, Sin conjured a mana storm that sealed the island off; no scrying, no teleport, no signal in or out. Caelin, who had foreseen the catastrophe, had already sent fifty adventurers, Expedition Alpha, when the storm was beginning—to secure the crystal for himself under the guise of evacuating civilians.

During the operation, four of Alpha, Jack, Cassidy, Alleria, and Kain, found the truth: the crystal is one of the fonts that hold the world’s ancient enemy at bay, and a dark influence within it was twisting their desires. Jack became certain he alone could protect the island; Alleria fixated on experimenting on the crystal; Kain insisted on the mission above all and dismissed the civilians; Cassidy broke and wanted only to run. As they argued, the crystal’s influence pulsed outward. Sin dropped the whole island into the deep to drown the threat, to which the crystal raised a bubble of air around the city, keeping the water at bay. Whether out of love or possessive greed, it kept the people and the font together. Time inside the bubble runs twelve times faster than on the surface: three years have passed below, three months above.

## The city and the Council of Six

Sindral is divided into six districts, each with a councillor:

| District    | Councillor   | Notes |
|------------|--------------|--------|
| **Industrial** | Stelle Irre | Workshops, forges, crystal-fed machinery; backbone of production. |
| **Arcane**     | Axis        | Colleges, libraries, mages; the city’s magical heart. |
| **Evergreen**  | Elarion     | Parks, groves, and underground farms; food and air. |
| **Royal**      | Lancaster   | Old palace, nobility, ceremonial authority. |
| **Trade**      | Eywin       | Docks (now dead water), warehouses, merchants. |
| **Southedge**  | Sancho      | Military. |

After the drop, the Council of Six began to ration supplies, keep order, and decide whether to seek a way out or dig in. The split was immediate in spirit: **Eywin, Lancaster, and Sancho** wanted to find a way to pierce the bubble; **Stelle, Axis, and Elarion** argued that survival meant accepting the bubble and making the city self-sustaining, even if it slowly decayed.

## Timeline (inside the bubble / outside)

**Year 0, Month 0 — The drop & the council** *(Surface: Month 0)*  

The island sinks. For a few hours there is panic—flooding at the edges, screams, the sky replaced by the underside of the sea. Then the bubble stabilises: a dome of breathable air, lit by the crystal’s glow and by whatever magelight and industry still function. Expedition Alpha is presumed lost in the collapse. Jack, King, and Alleria do not correct that story; they fade into the crowds. Cassidy, uncorrupted by the crystal’s grip and believing Alpha dead, stays in the city and helps where they can. Staying under Lancaster's manor.

The Council of Six imposes rationing, curfews, and district-level responsibility for food, power, and order. The first priority is counting what’s left: stores, conduits, and the integrity of the bubble. Rumours spread that the council was hoarding the majority of the rations; the council insists it is holding the city safe.

**Months 1–6 — The “peace”** *(Surface: ~2 weeks)*  

Rationing holds. The Evergreen district’s farms and groves feed the city; Industrial keeps forges and conduits running; Arcane maintains the wards and the few communication arrays that still work. The council meets weekly. The divide hardens: Eywin, Lancaster, and Sancho push for expeditions to the bubble’s edge, for any crack in the bubble. Stelle, Axis, and Elarion refuse to risk the bubble or waste resources on “fantasy.” Tensions spill into shouting matches and delayed votes.

Unbeknownst to the council, Jack has reunited the surviving members of Alpha and bent them to his will. They begin small-scale sabotage—spoiled stores, “accidental” fires, burst pipes, rumours that one district is hoarding or another is plotting. The council blames bad luck, incompetence, or each other. Jack, Kain, and Alleria stay in the background; Kain is already looking for a way to get word to Caelin, while Alleria seeks any path back to the crystal chamber.

**Months 6–9 — Fracture** *(Surface: ~Month 0.5–0.75)*  

Sabotage escalates. Key conduits fail; warehouses burn; Evergreen’s irrigation lines are cut. The council accuses one another of hoarding or deliberate wrecking. Lancaster points at Trade; Eywin points at Royal; Sancho’s Southedge is accused of hiding supplies. Districts begin to withhold food and parts from the central pool. Rations shrink. The first deaths from starvation and untreated illness are reported; the dead are buried in Evergreen or dropped into the dark water at the bubble’s edge.

Jack’s faction remains hidden. Alpha strikes at night and leaves no clear trail. Kain continues to hunt for a way to send a message through the storm; Alleria gets closer to the crystal, bribing or threatening guards and scholars. The city no longer feels like six camps sharing a dome.

**Months 9–12 — Open rebellion** *(Surface: ~Month 0.75–1)*  

Axis, Cassidy, a core of magical college students, and farmers from Evergreen’s outskirts lead the first open uprising. Their demand: end the hoarding and the infighting; restore fair distribution; purge the council of those who “starved the city.” The first major battle is fought at a contested granary. **Axis is killed by Alleria** — whether by loyalist troops, Alpha, or crossfire is still debated. His death turns him into a martyr; the rebellion spreads district by district. Any attempt to talk and restore democracy to the city is shut down with an attempted assassination.

Lancaster, Eywin, and Sancho try to hold their territories with militia and remaining loyalists. Stelle and Elarion are caught between the rebels and the “way out” bloc. Jack, King, and Alleria finally step into the open: Alpha and their allies seize bridges, forges, and the approaches to the crystal. Cassidy and Stelle, unwilling to serve either side, go into hiding. Cassidy in the ruins of the Arcane quarter, Stelle in the Industrial underbelly. Elarion is captured by Jack’s forces and forced to appear at council meetings as a figurehead. The rebellion has no single leader after Axis’s death; it fragments into cells and neighbourhood militias.

**Months 12–18 — The war year** *(Surface: ~Month 1–1.5)*  
City-wide fighting. During the chaos someone lets the zoo animals loose; phase spiders later build a web bridge across the valley that becomes a rebel route. The land along the approach to the Industrial District is burned and rots from within, becoming a toxic plague wasteland. Jack, King, and Alleria systematically remove the three “way out” councillors: **Lancaster, Eywin, and Sancho** are killed and replaced by Jack, King, and Alleria. Resistance cells persist but lack a single leader. The nominal “Council of Six” is reassembled under Jack’s control: Jack, King, Alleria, a coerced **Stelle**, a captive **Elarion**, and a new sixth—**Grubbus** (Fizzbank goblin). By the end of the rebellion (~Month 18), Jack effectively rules most of the city.

**Months 18–36 — Jack’s reign** *(Surface: ~Month 1.5–3)*  

A year and a half of brittle order. Jack’s regime runs on rationing, informants, and the threat of Alpha. Public dissent is crushed; the council meets but only Jack’s bloc decides. **Lancaster Mansion** is used as an Alpha base and holds archaeologists: **Dr. Elizabeth Irre** (Stelle’s sister) is kept in the basement and allowed to research a way out of the bubble. Something happens to the outposts along the road toward the Industrial District. The farms and villages in the plague lands had become a rest stop after the sinking. A **smuggler** takes supplies from the farmers’ village (Moonshine). The green crystal used in weapons has been **infecting** people; one ex‑Alpha member, **the Abhorrant**, puts infected people down at the cost of his own arm, goes crazy, and goes into hiding.

**Stelle**, no longer willing to play the coerced councillor, and begins rebuilding the Industrial District from the underbelly, reclaiming her factories, rallying survivors and trash collectors into the Society of Trash, and eventually securing a fragment of the Conjuration crystal (the “Heart of the Island”) to power her warforged. Over the following year she turns Industrial into a fortified, semi-independent faction.

**Six months ago (Month 30)** Carol Wellspring and James Cassidy formally start the second rebellion. Carol, an investigative reporter turned organiser, and Cassidy, the only Alpha veteran free of Jack’s control and the rebellion’s military mind, establish Reach Outpost as the insurgency’s headquarters. They recruit from farmers, refugees, and anyone willing to resist; Cassidy is paid in exotic weaponry and crystal-charged gear (he later picks up his signature hat from the farmlands north, from a pile of skeletons). Many farmers distrust the rebellion because it has never followed up on its promises. The insurgency remains scattered but now has a public face and a base of operations.

Toward the end of this period, King, by tricking Alleria’s experiments, finds a way to get a message through the storm in a one-way burst; the surface receives word that Sindral is alive and that Alpha has taken control. **King Caelin** learns that an adventuring party from his realm has staged a hostile takeover. Fearing exposure and the wrath of other nations (and of the Stelvyrn Empire), he negotiates with the Empire and organises a second expedition - officially a “rescue mission” for the lost city. He sends his magician **Azrael Thornbringer** to coordinate with Kain and to ensure a cover-up: no one left alive in the bubble to contradict the story that the mana storm destroyed Sindral. The rescue ships (Beta, Gamma, Delta) set out.

## Current state (end of Month 36 / Surface Month 3)

The city is resentful and subdued. Jack rules through the puppet council from the Shard; Alpha enforces. **Stelle** has reclaimed the Industrial District and leads the Society of Trash and Stelle Industries, holding the Heart of the Island; she negotiates with Jack as a rival power. The **insurgency** is led by **Carol Wellspring** from Reach Outpost, with **James Cassidy** as second-in-command. Cassidy and scattered resistance cells operate in the outskirts and plague lands (the burnt, rotting wasteland along the road to Industrial). **The Abhorrant**, after an encounter with Azrael, has decided the rebellion endangers the “cold peace” of the occupation and uses broken warforged to block the rebellion from reaching the Industrial District and to set a trap for “the other survivor.” Elarion plays the part of a cooperating councillor. The Anchor still pulses beneath the city; Alleria likely has access. On the surface, the “rescue” expedition (Beta, Gamma, Delta) is under way; the black ship that went in first lies ripped in half in the bubble. The expedition’s real mission is to silence Sindral forever.
